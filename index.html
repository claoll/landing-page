Let's cook some canelés

A canelé is a small French pastry that originated in the Bordeaux region of France. It has a caramelized crust and a soft, custardy center, and is flavored with rum and vanilla.

Here it is the receipe to cook perfect canelés !

The receipe shawn in video

Ingredients to make about 18 medium-sized canelés

500 grams whole milk

50 grams butter

185 grams white granulated sugar

150 grams bread flour

3 egg yolks

50 grams cognac or dark rum

The steps

    Melt the butter and brush the insides of the molds with the melt butter.

    Whisk the yolks together with the sugar, salt, and sifted flour.

    Temper the egg yolks by whisking in 1/3 of the milk, then the another 1/3, then the last 1/3 of the mixture. At this point, the batter will be very liquid. Finally, whisk in the cognac.

    Pour the batter 4/5 up the mold and Bake the cannelés for 10 minutes at 450°F (232°C) then lower the temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue to bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour.

    Unmold the cannelés using tongs or a towel to slam the molds upside-down on a wire rack. And enjoy !
